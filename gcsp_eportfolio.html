About This Portfolio

This e-portfolio documents my journey through the Grand Challenges Scholars Program (GCSP). Here, you will find reflections on my competencies, examples of my projects, and highlights of my capstone work.

My goal is to showcase not only my technical skills but also my ability to collaborate across disciplines, think critically, and approach problems with creativity and global awareness.

Global/Cultural Competency

To develop my global and cultural competency, I plan to immerse myself in diverse experiences that broaden my worldview and enhance my ability to work effectively in multicultural settings. This includes language learning, cultural immersion, and community engagement. These steps will help me address global challenges with sensitivity to cultural nuances.

Enroll in Spanish Language Courses

Starting next semester, I'll take introductory Spanish classes at the University of Idaho to communicate better in diverse teams. This will prepare me for potential collaborations in Latin American tech projects.

Attend Global Cultural Festivals

I'll participate in events like the Moscow International Festival, capturing moments of dance and cuisine from around the world to build empathy and cultural appreciation.

Volunteer with Refugee Support Groups

Through local NGOs, I'll volunteer to assist immigrant families, learning about their stories and challenges to foster inclusive problem-solving in engineering.

Participate in University International Cultural Events

I'll attend and volunteer at intercultural events organized by the University of Idaho's International Programs, connecting with students from around the world to expand my global perspective.

Entrepreneurship/Innovation/Viable Business

My plan for developing entrepreneurship skills involves hands-on experiences in ideation, prototyping, and pitching viable business concepts. As a CS student, I'll focus on tech-driven innovations that align with grand challenges, such as sustainable software solutions.

Join University Startup Incubator

I'll enroll in the Vandal Startup program this year, attending workshops on business modeling to turn my app ideas into prototypes.

Prototype a Tech Startup Idea

Using tools like Figma and no-code platforms, I'll build an MVP for an AI-driven sustainability app, testing it with peers for feedback and iteration.

Complete Online Entrepreneurship Certification

Through Coursera's "Entrepreneurship" specialization, I'll learn lean startup methods and apply them to a personal project over the next six months.

Join the University of Idaho Entrepreneurship Club

I'll become an active member of the U of I Entrepreneurship Club, participating in discussions, guest speaker sessions, and collaborative idea sessions to build my network and skills.
